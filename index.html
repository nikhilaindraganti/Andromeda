About the Andromeda Galaxy

Introduction

The Andromeda Galaxy, also known as Messier 31, M31, or NGC 224, is a spiral galaxy approximately 2.537 million light-years away from Earth.

Characteristics

Andromeda is the largest galaxy in the Local Group, which also contains our Milky Way galaxy. It has a diameter of about 220,000 light-years and is thought to contain approximately 1 trillion stars.

Discovery

The Andromeda Galaxy was first described by Persian astronomer Abd al-Rahman al-Sufi in his Book of Fixed Stars around 964 AD. It was independently rediscovered by several other astronomers in the 17th century.
